## Overview
Scout Delgado is the ethical voice and skilled technician in the student contractor team building the neural transmitter. He voices the group's moral risks and technical doubts, serving as the story's "what if we stop?" perspective. 

His arc humanizes the human cost of complicity—technical talent clashing with growing guilt. 

## Profile
Scout hunches over work with frantic precision, hands snapping between components. He's the team's best drone/logistics coder, but fixates on downstream harm. 

Post-payment, guilt drives his push to quit, making him the fracture point. 

## Appearance
Early-20s, sweatshirt sleeves pushed up, frantic lab posture. Tired eyes from all-nighters, chaotic penciled notes. No major augments noted—earns via skill. 

## Personality
Principled but overwhelmed—questions ethics openly, but folds under group pressure/money need. Post-crisis trembling hands betray fear. 

## Motivations
Escape poverty via drone networks (aid delivery bypassing corps/gov). Fears becoming accomplice to harm. 

## Strengths and flaws
Strengths: rapid problem-solving, drone/logistics coding, moral clarity. 

Flaws: hesitation under pressure, debt-driven vulnerability to temptation. 

## Relationships
Vitaliy: resists pressure, calls out risks. Britney: allies in fear/debt. Zhiming: pragmatic foil to his caution.

## Character arc
Hesitant participation → moral crisis post-attacks → attempt to stop (fails/captured). Mirror for protagonists' compromises. 

## Themes
Guilt of complicity, good intentions enabling harm, desperation vs. principle.
