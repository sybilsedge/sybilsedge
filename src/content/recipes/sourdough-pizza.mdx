## Ingredients

### Dough
- 150 g (⅔ cup) active sourdough starter, 100% hydration
- 250 g (1 cup + 1 tbsp) water, room temperature
- 400 g (3⅓ cups) bread flour
- 10 g (1¾ tsp) fine sea salt
- 5 g (1 tsp) olive oil

### Sauce
- 1 can (400 g) whole San Marzano tomatoes, crushed by hand
- 2 cloves garlic, microplaned
- ½ tsp dried oregano
- Pinch of red pepper flakes
- Salt to taste

### Toppings
- 150 g low-moisture whole-milk mozzarella, torn
- Fresh basil, added after the bake
- Good olive oil, to finish

## Instructions

### Day before — mix and cold-ferment

1. Whisk starter and water together until uniform. Add flour and salt; mix until no dry flour remains.
2. Rest 30 minutes (autolyse), then fold in olive oil with a few stretch-and-folds.
3. Perform 3 sets of coil folds over 90 minutes, then divide into 2 balls.
4. Cover and refrigerate 8–24 hours. Longer = more complexity.

### Day of — preheat and bake

1. Pull dough from fridge 2 hours before baking to take the chill off.
2. Place cast iron skillet on the center rack. Preheat oven to 500°F (260°C) for at least 45 minutes.
3. Stretch dough on a lightly floured surface — work from the center outward, letting gravity do the thinning. Rest 15 minutes if it snaps back.
4. Spoon a thin layer of sauce over the dough, leaving a 1-inch border.
5. Scatter mozzarella over the sauce.
6. Carefully transfer pizza to the screaming-hot skillet (a peel or parchment helps).
7. Bake 12–15 minutes until the crust is deep amber at the edge and the cheese has golden spots.
8. Rest 3 minutes, finish with basil and a drizzle of olive oil. Cut with scissors for clean slices.

## Notes

- **Starter health:** This dough relies on active starter — use it 4–8 hours after feeding when it's domed and bubbly.
- **Flour:** Bread flour gives better structure than all-purpose. 00 flour is authentic but harder to source.
- **No peel?** Par-bake the raw pizza on a sheet pan for 5 minutes, then transfer to the cast iron for the last 8.
- **Broiler finish:** If the top isn't coloring fast enough, switch to broil for the final 2 minutes — watch it closely.
